02Sell-through rate · 2024 vs 2025

Who is failing
the threshold?

A healthy garment group sells 70% or more of what it receives. Five groups clear it easily. One does not, in either year.

Sell-through rate, by group
% of received units sold · Sep to Nov average
2024 2025
29%
Womenswear Fashion sits at 22 to 37% across all six data points. Every other group meets or exceeds the 70% line. This is not a company-wide problem. It is a single garment group failing, repeatedly, in both years.
03Volume comparison · Received vs Sold

How big is the gap?

The wider the distance between received and sold, the more unsold stock sitting in the warehouse. Most groups stay close. One opens a chasm.

Units received vs units sold
Combined totals across Sep–Nov, both years
Received Sold
8,400
Womenswear Fashion received over 12,000 units across the Sep to Nov window in both years combined. It sold roughly 3,600. That is around 8,400 unsold units, from a single garment group alone.
04Net stock movement · Year over year

Did anything
change in 2025?

Net stock movement is units received minus units sold. A flat line is healthy. A high, repeating line is a structural problem the buyers have not corrected.

Womenswear Fashion · Unsold units per month
2024 vs 2025 · September, October, November
2024 2025
−1.7%
Sales fell slightly in 2025. Womenswear Fashion sold 1,770 units across Sep to Nov 2025 vs 1,800 in 2024. Receiving dropped by the same 30 units, so the unsold pile stayed almost identical at ~4,517 units per year. The pattern repeats. Nothing structural improved.
