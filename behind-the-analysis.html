02The pipeline

Three steps,
six queries.

Enrich. Aggregate. Join with derived metrics. The whole pipeline lives inside BigQuery as four CREATE OR REPLACE statements.

01
Enrich the raw tables.

WMS and POS only carry SKU codes. The first step is attaching department and product type metadata from Product Master to both, using a LEFT JOIN on SKU. This metadata then carries through the rest of the pipeline.

wms_enriched · attach product metadata to stock received BigQuery
CREATE OR REPLACE TABLE `Project1_Retail.wms_enriched` AS WITH pm AS (SELECT * FROM `Project1_Retail.project_master`), wms AS (SELECT * FROM `Project1_Retail.wms_stock_received`) SELECT pm.SKU, pm.Department, pm.Product_Type, pm.Product_Name, wms.Units_Received, wms.Date AS Received_Date FROM pm LEFT JOIN wms ON pm.SKU = wms.SKU
pos_enriched · same pattern, applied to POS BigQuery
CREATE OR REPLACE TABLE `Project1_Retail.pos_enriched` AS WITH pm AS (SELECT * FROM `Project1_Retail.project_master`), pos AS (SELECT * FROM `Project1_Retail.pos_stock_sold`) SELECT pm.SKU, pm.Department, pm.Product_Type, pm.Product_Name, pos.Units_Sold, pos.Date AS Selling_Date FROM pm LEFT JOIN pos ON pm.SKU = pos.SKU
02
Aggregate by group and month.

Daily SKU records become monthly garment-group totals. UNION ALL stacks all six month and year combinations into one aggregated table for both WMS and POS, sidestepping the cartesian product risk that comes with joining non-aggregated tables directly.

pos_total · UNION ALL across six month and year windows BigQuery
CREATE OR REPLACE TABLE `Project1_Retail.pos_total` AS SELECT Department, Product_Type, 'September' AS Month, 2025 AS Year, SUM(Units_Sold) AS Total_Units_Sold FROM `Project1_Retail.pos_enriched` WHERE Selling_Date BETWEEN '2025-09-01' AND '2025-09-30' GROUP BY Department, Product_Type UNION ALL -- pattern continues for Oct 2025, Nov 2025, Sep / Oct / Nov 2024 SELECT Department, Product_Type, 'November' AS Month, 2024 AS Year, SUM(Units_Sold) AS Total_Units_Sold FROM `Project1_Retail.pos_enriched` WHERE Selling_Date BETWEEN '2024-11-01' AND '2024-11-30' GROUP BY Department, Product_Type
03
Final overstock_analysis table.

The final query joins wms_total and pos_total on three keys: garment group, month, year. CONCAT builds a single garment_group identifier from Department and Product_Type. WMS is the left table because any stock not received has no business in a sales analysis. Sell-through rate and net stock movement are derived in the same pass.

overstock_analysis · final table, all derived metrics BigQuery
CREATE OR REPLACE TABLE `Project1_Retail.overstock_analysis` AS WITH wms AS ( SELECT CONCAT(Department, ' ', Product_Type) AS Garment_Group, Month, Year, Total_Units_Received FROM `Project1_Retail.wms_total` ), pos AS ( SELECT CONCAT(Department, ' ', Product_Type) AS Garment_Group, Month, Year, Total_Units_Sold FROM `Project1_Retail.pos_total` ) SELECT wms.Month, wms.Year, wms.Garment_Group, wms.Total_Units_Received, pos.Total_Units_Sold, Total_Units_Received - Total_Units_Sold AS Net_Stock_Movement, Total_Units_Sold / Total_Units_Received * 100 AS sell_through_rate FROM wms LEFT JOIN pos ON wms.Garment_Group = pos.Garment_Group AND wms.Month = pos.Month AND wms.Year = pos.Year
03Analytical decisions

Four calls
worth defending.

Every analysis hides a stack of choices that did not make it into the final chart. Here are mine.

Why sell-through rate as the primary metric?
It normalises volume between groups. A department receiving 2,000 units and selling 400 looks very different from one receiving 800 and selling 750. Sell-through rate surfaces that contrast immediately, at 20% vs 94%.
Why WMS as the left table?
Anything not received cannot be sold. Anchoring on WMS ensures the analysis only covers stock that actually entered the system. A WMS row without a matching POS record is itself the overstock signal.
Why UNION ALL over a single GROUP BY?
Pre-aggregating each month and year window before the final join eliminates cartesian product risk. Joining non-aggregated daily records would multiply rows unpredictably and quietly inflate every metric downstream.
Why scope to Sep to Nov?
This is the pre-holiday window where demand softens before Black Friday. It is the period most vulnerable to overstock accumulation, and where buying decisions have the largest knock-on effect on end-of-year clearance.
